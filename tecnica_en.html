The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method created by Francesco Cirillo in the late 80's. Its purpose is provide an easy way to focus and improve productivity.

Its name comes from the Italian word pomodoro (which means tomato), in reference to the shape of the kitchen timer Cirillo used when he developed the technique.

The method consists of a series of time intervals (generally 25 minutes). These intervals are called 'pomodoros'. One pomodoro should be used to perform a task without interruption. After finishing a pomodoro you should take a break.

The technique can be summarized in 5 steps:

1
Seleccionar una tarea a realizar
2
Programar el temporizador (generalmente se establecen lapsos de 25 minutos de trabajo)
3
Work without interruption until the lapse is completed
4
Take a 5 minute break
5
Se inicia un nuevo lapso de trabajo (pomodoro), cuando se completan 4 pomodoros, en lugar de tomar un descanso corto, se toma un descanso largo de 25 minutos
